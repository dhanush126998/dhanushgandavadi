ABOUT

Bioengineering at the interface of biomaterials, molecular design, and cancer biology.

I am a graduate researcher in bioengineering developing experimental platforms that make complex biological processes measurable, tunable, and therapeutically actionable.

Research approach

My work combines biomaterials, DNA nanotechnology, three-dimensional cell culture, quantitative imaging, and computational analysis. I am particularly interested in engineering systems that recreate biological organization while remaining experimentally controlled and reproducible.

Across my projects, I focus on building tools that do more than reproduce a biological phenomenon. I aim to create platforms that enable mechanistic discovery, isolate meaningful cellular subpopulations, and support translational applications.
