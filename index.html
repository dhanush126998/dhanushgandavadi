Ph.D. in Bioengineering

A blend of DNA, bioengineering and some madness.

I'm a bioengineer who enjoys turning ambitious ideas into experimental reality. My research has never been about solving one biological problem at a time. Instead, it has been driven by a broader question: Can DNA serve as a universal engineering material for biology? This vision has led me to develop programmable DNA nanostructures that interact with cells and biomolecules to create new technologies for cancer therapeutics, virus inhibition, immune sensing, and beyond.
